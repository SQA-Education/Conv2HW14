Dialogue · The Formal Proposal

Persuasion & Professional Advocacy

Maria is a senior warehouse associate. Robert is the Warehouse Manager.

Maria: Robert, do you have a moment? I want to touch base about the new safety framework.

Robert: Of course, Maria. I know the team has been busy. Do you have a suggestion?

Maria: I do. Currently, the night shift is struggling to finish the inventory. I've spoken to the team, and there is a consensus that we need a better system. If we hired a part-time loader for the 6:00 PM transition, the morning shift would be much more productive.

Robert: I understand, but the budget is tight this month.

Maria: I appreciate the budget concerns, but this is a safety issue. It is essential that the warehouse floor remain clear of empty pallets overnight. Right now, workers are rushing and cutting corners.

Robert: That's a compelling point. I don't want anyone getting injured.

Maria: Exactly. It's a win-win situation: the store stays in compliance with safety rules, and we stop losing time on the morning shift.

Robert: I'll look into the budget today. I suggest that you meet with me tomorrow morning to discuss the final decision.

3 · Write A Proposal to Your Manager

Think about a specific change you'd like to see at your current or past job (better equipment, a change in shift times, a more organized breakroom). Write a 4–6 sentence email or proposal to your manager. Clearly state how the change benefits the store or the team.

📜 Subjunctive mood — formal requests use the base verb (no -s, no "to"):
"I propose that the company hire a loader."
"It is essential that the floor be clear."
"I suggest that she call the desk."

💬 Try to use: framework, consensus, compromise, effective, objection, back someone up, on the same page.

  • 4–6 sentences, written as an email or proposal
  • Use the subjunctive mood at least once (I propose that…, It is essential that…)
  • At least 2 lesson vocabulary words
  • State the benefit for the store or the team
